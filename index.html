Part 01Competitor Benchmark Analysis

How Trendyol, Noon and Jumia build dedicated deals landing pages that intentionally break from their main store design to signal "savings mode" and drive higher conversion rates.

Trendyol

Turkey's #1 ecommerce — gamified deal discovery

Page TypeCategory-driven deals hub with flash product sections
Flash SalesTime-slotted at 6 h, 9 h, 12 h, 15 h, 18 h intervals
NavigationGender segmentation + category tabs
UrgencyTime-slot scheduling, rotating deals
CouponsCollect up to 5 / day, stackable with deals
ColorsNeutral whites + teal CTAs + blue accents
MobileMobile-first, app push alerts for deals
PersonalizationA/B testing + geo-based content
Key Insight: Deals as gamified time-gated events with collectible coupons — drives repeat visits.

Noon

MENA leader — event-driven mega sale identity

Page TypeEvent-driven mega sale (Yellow Friday / Flash Sales)
Flash Sales6-hour and 12-hour refreshing flash deals
HeroFull-width yellow banners, bold discount %
UrgencyCountdown timers + limited stock messaging
ExclusiveNoon One member pricing, 0 % installments
SocialShareable curated wishlists
ColorsYellow dominates deals (breaks from standard blue)
BundlesBundle deals, Buy X Get Y promotions
Key Insight: Creates entirely separate branded experiences for deals — yellow signals "savings mode" to users.

Jumia

Africa's marketplace — urgency-maximized deal pages

Page TypeMulti-section hub: flash sales + category deals
Flash SalesHH:MM:SS countdown + upcoming schedule visible
HeroWarm beige / tan hero with seasonal themes
StockExact count: "56 left", "1 item left"
FilteringBrand, Price, Size, Gender, Discount tier, Rating
BadgesFree Shipping, Made in Egypt, Official Store
ColorsWarm neutrals + orange CTAs + red badges
GridResponsive cards with sponsored product slots
Key Insight: Maximum urgency via exact stock counts + timers + discount tier filters for bargain hunting.

Key Patterns Across All Three

🎨

Separate Visual Identity

All three use distinct colors / layouts for deals that break from their main store

⏱️

Countdown Timers

Visible HH:MM:SS timers on flash deals create urgency to buy now

📉

Stock Scarcity

Exact items-left count or progress bars showing stock depletion

🏷️

Discount Tier Filters

Users filter by discount % (10 %, 20 %, 30 %, 50 %+) to find best deals

🔄

Flash Rotation

Deals rotate every 6-12 hours creating "come back" behaviour

📱

Mobile-First + Sticky CTA

All pages mobile-optimised with sticky add-to-cart buttons

🔥

Warm Urgency Colors

Orange, red, yellow used across all three for deal-specific pages

🏅

Trust Badges

Official store, free shipping, authentic product badges near CTA

🎟️

Coupon / Member Exclusives

Special pricing for loyalty members creates VIP feeling

🗂️

Category Quick-Access

Icon-based category tiles at top for fast navigation within deals

💡

The #1 Pattern: Break from Main Store

All three competitors create visually distinct deal experiences. A different look signals "special event" and puts users in buying mode.

Wireframe Annotations

→ Sticky Countdown Header

Global deal timer always visible. Red pulsing animation draws attention. Creates persistent urgency.

→ Hero Banner

Full-width gradient hero. Uses warm orange-to-gold gradient. Must look NOTHING like the main JustYol store — it's a "deal event" identity.

→ Flash Deals + Stock Bars

Horizontally scrollable on mobile. Color-coded stock progress bars (red = almost gone, orange = going fast). #1 urgency driver from Jumia.

→ Discount Tier Filter Pills

Scrollable pill filters let users hunt for the deepest discounts. Pattern from Jumia.

→ Coupon Zone

Dedicated coupon section with copy-to-clipboard. Inspired by Trendyol's collectible coupon system.

→ Flash Schedule + Reminder

Shows upcoming flash sale times. "Set Reminder" captures push notification opt-in — drives return visits.

→ Sticky Bottom CTA (Mobile)

Always-visible cart bar with item count and running total. One-tap access to checkout. Critical for mobile CR.
